Benny's Adventure

A mockup-toy meant to help a 3 year old boy named Benny with his occupational therapy. Assistive technology can come in all shapes and forms, we just have to go out and make it!

-Arduino -Circuits -LaserCutting -AssistiveTech

Video of the project.



Behind the Scenes

This project was a collaboration between Ruyi Chen and I.

This project was done in a class which focused on assistive technology at NYU ITP. We met with an occupational therapist whom challenged us to make therapy fun for a patient of her's named Benny. Benny was 3 years old at the time, and had Cerebral Palsy.

We were given a cardboard box compatible with his sitting chair. Benny's occupational therapy was comprised of him pressing his feet into the cardboard box which had a switch inside.

Using this cardboard switch, we crafted a toy that had lights and music. It showed Benny going up and down a belt, as if flying. The toy came with multiple backgrounds and cutouts of his whole family.

Floor Gallery

A temporary installation in which users walk across a floor to discover the art piece being projected. In the dark, as you move, only the user can bring the art to life.

-Arduino -Processing/Java -Computer Vision -Photoshop

Video demonstration of the project.



Behind the Scenes

This project was a collaborative project between Quinny He and I.

Using an IR camera mounted above, and therefore computer vision in processing, we tracked a user as they walked across the floor. A projector was mounted above as well, so as the user was tracked, the image of "the Kiss" by Klimt was displayed bit by bit. The installation took place in a completely dark room, and the ambiance created brought a calm to all of us.

This idea was meant to be a prototype of a whole musuem that would function this way. Various famous paintings displayed, and could only be fully realized by users exploring in the dark.
